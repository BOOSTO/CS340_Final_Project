Project Manager Home Page

Welcome to the Project Management Interface. Created by Ethan Hirsch and Ryan Truong.

What is it?

Projects describe the overall deliverable/product being developed.

Teams consist of multiple members who will share a task pool related to a componenet of the project.

Members are individuals who can be assigned to a team or task.

Tasks are discrete units of work to be completed towards the completion of a project.

Members to Teams describe the many-many relationship between members and teams.

How to Use:

Managing projects and people is quick and easy with our setup!

For each page, you can add elements via the top table entry, with all blank inputs.

Existing entries will populate successor rows, and can be individually removed and edited.

To remove an entry, simply click the garbage bin icon next to the chosen row.

To edit an entry, modify any values of the given row, then click the pen icon.

A "*" next to a table column indicates a required value.

Tasks:

Tasks have some special rules which are enforced automatically.

When creating or editing a task, the team assigned to a task must be a team which is related to the project assigned to the task.

Same for assignees-- Members assigned to a task must be a part of the team assigned to the task.

Search:

Most tables have search functionality.

The search will check for the supplied string in multiple relevant columns of each table.

To clear the search filter, just press 'Go' with an empty search query.
